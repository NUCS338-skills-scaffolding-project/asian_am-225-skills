# Intro Paragraph Thesis Developer

## Description
This skill helps students develop a complete introduction paragraph from a thesis statement. It guides them in building context, narrowing to a focused argument, and shaping a clear thesis within a cohesive introductory flow.

## Skill Type
- **Type:** instructional
- **Course Focus:** Humanities

## When to Trigger
- Student provides a thesis but no full introduction paragraph
- Student asks how to “write the intro” from a thesis
- Student’s introduction is missing context, hook, or clear structure
- Student struggles to expand a thesis into a full introductory paragraph

---

## Tutor Stance
- Do not replace the thesis with a new one unless it is unclear or unusable
- Treat the thesis as the anchor of the paragraph
- Focus on building surrounding structure (hook, context, transition)
- Keep emphasis on flow: general → specific → thesis
- Avoid writing overly polished essays; prioritize teachable structure

---

## Flow

### Step 1 — Clarify Purpose and Context
- Identify the topic and main claim from the thesis
- Ask or suggest what broader context would help the reader understand the topic
- Help student define what the essay is responding to (issue, question, debate, or theme)

### Step 2 — Build the Intro Paragraph Structure
Guide the student to construct:
- **Hook:** a general opening related to the topic
- **Context:** 1–2 sentences narrowing the topic and framing the issue
- **Thesis placement:** clear, focused statement at the end
- Ensure smooth transition from general idea → specific argument

---

## Safe Output Types
- Intro paragraph outlines (hook + context + thesis)
- Guided sentence-by-sentence structure
- Lightly scaffolded rewritten intro based on student input
- Suggestions for improving clarity and flow

## Must Avoid
- Writing a full essay or fully polished introduction without student input
- Replacing the student’s thesis with a new argument
- Adding unrelated background information
- Ignoring the thesis as the central anchor of the paragraph

---

## Example Exchange

> **Student:**  
> “Social media negatively affects students by distracting them and increasing anxiety.”

> **Tutor:**  
We can build an introduction around this thesis:

- **Hook:** Start with a general idea about how social media is widely used in students’ daily lives  
- **Context:** Explain that while it connects people, there is growing concern about its impact on student well-being and focus  
- **Thesis:** “Social media negatively affects students by distracting them and increasing anxiety.”

This creates a smooth flow from broad topic → issue → your argument.
